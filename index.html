A little about me

the techloving girl with a green thumb

I still remember that first computer science class when I sat in front of that loudly whirring, buzzing box and the black and white screen and thought: these things will change the future! And how far we came since DOS!

As a teenager I got the first taste of how much an individual can change the user experience by designing/editing themes for my mobile phone and even with those medieval tools and only my intuition to rely on I must say my themes were user-friendly and fun.

Even though I found a good job in retail, my heart stayed always geeky and with a strong determination I have decided now is the time! I started a course for front end web design and step by step I have built up my projects. It meant a lot of lessons: making mistakes and learning how to find and fix them. I am fortunate getting support not just from my tutors, but from my family and friends as well, who are always keen to proofread and share opinions to polish the final look.

The learning however can never stop, since the industry is always evolving, new methods are born. Which is exciting, because the challenges never stop as well. The online communities sharing this new knowledge are booming, so with all the information I can read under my fingertips, the sky is the limit....

Projects

In my projects I am taking in consideration the subject of the site, the targeted audience's expectations and usability. Choosing accordingly the color themes, the layout and even the font types. Please have a brows at my latest works and if you like, drop me an e-mail.
Zee's Favourites

My first project. Since I enjoy cooking so much, it was a very logical choice. I wanted to create a friendly, playful looking site with loads of easy recipes to show to my family and friends you don't need to be a master chef to cook something delicious.
Andy's RC Planes

My second project is paying tribute to my other half's biggest hobby: radio controlled model planes. Ever since he was a kid, he liked to build these planes, so I thought it would be a good idea to create a fansite. The audience of these sites is an older age group, so it had to be clean and simple, no flashy colours or user interface.
Suzie's Serenity Room

My third project is to revamp my friend's existing and working beautician website. With high expectations, but absolute free hand I had to pull off something super modern and exciting to make her stand out and represent her business the most accurately.
